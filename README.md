💭 ☕ Morning Coffee Thoughts - “Full stack” doesn’t mean what it used to anymore 👀 Agree❓  
Across a lot of the roles I’m hiring for right now, it’s coming up constantly but not in the way people think. No one’s really looking for perfect 50/50 frontend/backend engineers.

It’s more:
💭 Engineers with a clear strength, who can take something from idea → live product without waiting on 3 other teams

From a hiring perspective, the shift is pretty clear 🔍
💭 Teams are smaller, so each hire needs to cover more ground
less appetite for siloed engineers
💭 Companies are prioritising people who can own features end-to-end

So the profiles getting the most traction tend to be 🎯
💭 Strong in one area (backend or frontend)
💭 Comfortable jumping across the stack when needed
💭 Used to owning and shipping features, not just contributing
💭 Able to work closely with product / design

And something I’m hearing more and more from clients 👇
💭 They want to see clear evidence of this.
Not just “full stack” on a CV, but:
💭 Examples of taking something 0→1
💭 Owning a feature from idea through to production
💭 Being able to talk through decisions and trade-offs
If a candidate can’t point to that, it’s usually where processes fall down.

And something newer that’s coming up a lot 🤖
💭 How you use AI in your day-to-day.
Not building models, but how you actually work with tools like GitHub Copilot or Claude to:
⏩  Move faster
⏩  Unblock yourself
⏩  Iterate quickly
That’s becoming a real differentiator in processes.

Feels like “full stack” now is less about the stack itself, and more about:
ownership + flexibility + how quickly you can execute

Interested if others are seeing the same?
